Part 3: The CBD Family Tree

This family is known for its therapeutic benefits without a significant "high." They interact with the body in more subtle ways than the THC family.

CBD

What it is:

The most famous non-psychoactive compound.

How it works:

Interacts with serotoninA neurotransmitter that helps regulate mood, happiness, and anxiety. CBD's interaction with its receptors is key to its calming effects. and vanilloid receptors rather than binding strongly to CB1.

Potential Uses:

  • Anxiety
  • Inflammation
  • Pain & Seizures

CBG

What it is:

The "Mother" molecule in its active, non-acidic form.

How it works:

Can interact directly with both CB1 and CB2 receptorsPart of the endocannabinoid system, found mainly in the immune system and peripheral tissues. They are involved in regulating inflammation and pain. without causing a high.

Potential Uses:

  • Gut Health (IBD)
  • Antibacterial
  • Neuroprotection

CBN

What it is:

A mildly psychoactive compound formed as THC degrades over time.

How it works:

Has a weak affinity for the CB1 receptor, about 10% that of THC.

Potential Uses:

  • Sedation / Sleep
  • Antibacterial
  • Appetite Stimulation

CBC

What it is:

A common but non-psychoactive cannabinoid.

How it works:

Binds poorly to CB1, but interacts with other receptors linked to pain perception (TRPV1, TRPA1).

Potential Uses:

  • Anti-inflammatory
  • Pain Relief
  • NeurogenesisThe process by which new neurons (brain cells) are formed in the brain.

CBDV

What it is:

The "Varin" variant of CBD, with a different chemical tail.

How it works:

Mechanism is still under research, but it shows potential in modulating brain activity.

Potential Uses:

  • Seizure Disorders
  • Autism Symptoms
  • Nausea

CBDA

What it is:

The raw, acidic precursor to CBD, found in living cannabis plants.

How it works:

Acts as a COX-2 inhibitorA substance that blocks the COX-2 enzyme, which is involved in inflammation and pain. Similar to how drugs like ibuprofen work. and interacts with serotonin receptors.

Potential Uses:

  • Anti-inflammatory
  • Nausea
  • Anxiety

Part 4: Understanding THC ConcentratesHighly potent cannabis extracts that have concentrated compounds like THC and terpenes. They are much stronger than raw cannabis flower.

Concentrates are made by isolating the most desirable parts of the plant. Below are common types.

Shatter

A hard, glass-like concentrate made using a solventA liquid (like butane or CO2) that dissolves cannabinoids and terpenes from the plant material..

Wax / Budder

Solvent-based extracts with a softer, opaque, and waxy texture.

Live Resin

A flavorful extract made from fresh-frozen plants to preserve terpenesAromatic compounds that give cannabis its scent and flavor. They also have their own therapeutic effects..

Kief

A simple, solventless powder made of collected trichomesThe tiny, crystal-like glands on cannabis flowers that produce cannabinoids and terpenes..

Hash

Kief that has been heated and pressed into a solid, dark, and potent block.

Rosin

A pure, solventless extract made by squeezing cannabis with heat and pressure.
